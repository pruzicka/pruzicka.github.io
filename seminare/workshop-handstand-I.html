Handstand I

I will teach you how to stand on your own hands and I believe that eventually everyone can learn how to do it. The aim of this workshop is not to do a handstand immediately but to learn how to learn it. This workshop is for beginners with no or very little experience with balancing on hands.

Personally, I started to exercise a handstand regularly in autumn 2013 when I was 39. I’m neither student of a circus school nor gymnastic genius training since childhood; I’m a normal guy who likes to move and decided to learn a handstand. I know what an adult needs, what problems they have, and most importantly I know how to deal with the problems.

Where have I learnt this?

At first I tried it myself but it didn’t work. I might have figured it out somehow eventually but it would take long. Therefore I was looking for a teacher.

To understand how to do a handstand properly, what are the methods used, and how to work with various problems, I started to look for the best. All of them can do a one arm handstand without a blink however each one of them teaches it differently, has a different approach and teaching methods.

Besides my own findings, it will be their teachings, knowledge and experiences that I’ll pass on to you.

Ido Portal

Movement teacher, a man who has changed my understanding of movement in general. Thanks to him I started to work on a handstand. He is my greatest inspiration and I am truly grateful that I can keep learning from him. Read more about Ido here.

Yuval Ayalon

One of the best handbalancers around. He’s been training gymnastics since his childhood, later worked as a professional acrobat in Las Vegas. He specializes in hand balancing. He has helped me very much with a handstand. More about Yuval here.

Yuri Marmerstein

He started exercising in his 18, he went in for a circus, hand balancing, and gymnastics. He will show you a lot of things that he can also teach you. He has showed me a different approach to teaching and new methods of teaching a handstand and other skills.

What are we going to learn?

  • How to prepare and strengthen wrists
  • We will improve a straight position of the body, the line
  • We will discuss instruments and methods to relax shoulders
  • Special exercises for strengthening the back
  • Relaxing shoulder blades and rhomboid muscles
  • How and why exercise against the wall
  • ... and a lot more

I will provide you with “instruments” for exercising at home. If you are afraid of a handstand, if you don’t know where to start, or if you’re trying it and you’re not sure how to proceed - join us and I will be happy to teach you.

Whether you spend your time practicing asanas, skiing, playing volleyball, or going to the gym; handstand will change your life. It will improve your coordination, feeling of your own body in a space, and as a bonus you will have stronger wrists and relaxed shoulders and thoracic spine. And I mustn’t forget - standing on hands is fun!
